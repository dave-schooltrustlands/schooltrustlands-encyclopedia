# Banner credits — v56 (2026-05-14)

The Library's room banners are hand-drafted plan-view illustrations, one per
room, in the visual register of the Spatial Discovery Blueprint at
`/about/how-this-works/`. All twenty-three new banners (plus the Reading
Room banner shipped in v52) are AI-generated against a master style
prompt; provenance and source files preserved in substrate.

## v56 update — 2026-05-14

Twenty-three new plan-view banner illustrations installed across all rooms
in the Library — both the four live library rooms (Atlas, Counting House,
Map Room — Reading Room already had its plan-view from v52) and the rooms
that had been carrying placeholder LOC photos (Newsroom, Voices), plus
eighteen new banners for the Library's adjunct spaces (Great Hall,
Updates Room, Pro Wing) and the rooms being planned (Records Room, Court
Room, Breach & Recovery, Lecture Hall, The Stacks, Schoolroom, Founders'
Cabinet, Chronicle Room, Reference Desk, Computer Room) plus five reserved
banners not yet linked from the public site (Donor's Hall, Press Room,
Periodicals, Garden, Workshop).

The five Library-of-Congress photographs that were serving as Atlas,
Counting House, Map Room, Newsroom, and Voices banners through v55 have
been moved to `_archive/` — preserved, not removed, so the LOC references
remain discoverable for future scholarship.

## v10 update — 2026-05-08 (archived)

Through v55, six signature banner images served the four library rooms
plus Newsroom and Voices, all sourced from the Library of Congress digital
collections. LOC photographs of this vintage are in the public domain (no
known restrictions on publication). Source links and photographer credits
preserved below for the record; the JPGs themselves are at
`_archive/<slug>_loc_photo.jpg`. The Reading Room LOC photograph is
